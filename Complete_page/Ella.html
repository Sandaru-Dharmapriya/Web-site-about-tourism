||ELLA||

Rich in biological richness, the region is home to a wide range of vegetation and fauna. Hills covered in cloud forests and tea plantations encircle Ella. Due to its elevation, the town experiences a colder climate than the lowlands around. Views across Sri Lanka's southern plains are possible from the Ella Gap. Even the light from the lighthouses along the southern coast can be seen on a beautiful morning. Ella has a variety of incredible activities, from scaling lush mountain peaks to leaping into cool waterfalls. After spending the day in the outdoors, visitors can unwind in the town's lively environment at one of the many fantastic cafes and eateries. The top activities to do when visiting Ella, Sri Lanka, are listed below. Ella is breathtakingly beautiful, with its waterfalls, vegetation, and hills. It offers views, landscapes, and natural experiences that have never been experienced before. Ella is the ideal location to visit if one needs to recharge their minds. It has a lot of well-known locations and has been a popular tourist destination for a while.








Nine Arches Railway Bridge

The town is most famous for its iconic nine arches railway bridge which is a fine example of colonial-era construction in Sri Lanka. A trek down the railway track to the bridge is a popular activity, especially around the time the train arrives. There are numerous quaint cafes, walking trails and guest houses nestled in the hills for a peaceful getaway.

Little Adam's Peak through bamboo forests

Hiking Little Adam’s Peak in Sri Lanka is one of the most epic things to do in Ella. The peak can be hiked easily without a guide, as there is a marked path that leads you up the mountain, with plenty of signposts to show you the way. The peak is not to be confused with Adam’s peak, which is a little way away and a considerably tougher and longer hike. It gets its name from Adam’s peak because this mountain replicates its shape, in a smaller form.

Ellawala Falls

The Ella - Wellawaya Road leads to Ellewala Ella, a little but stunning waterfall in the Monaragala District. About 5 miles from Wellawaya Town, this waterfall is tucked away in the woods. To get to the Rathmal Vehera Ancient Temple from Wellawaya, use the Ella Road about 3 kilometres. Take the road that is adjacent to the temple to go to the waterfall. According to reports, the final few kilometres are rugged and unsuitable for vehicles with little ground clearance. You might need a 44 or a 3 wheeler to get around. You must traverse the bush for a few hundred metres in order to reach the fall.

Ravana Cave

2 kilometres outside of Ella town, at a height of 1370 metres, is the Ravana Cave. It is one of Ella's most significant historical tourism destinations. According to legend, King Ravana utilised the Ravana cave to hide Princess Sita. The Ravana Cave must be visited if you have any interest in Sri Lankan history and are willing to walk some stairs to get there. It is unquestionably among the enjoyable things to do in Ella, Sri Lanka. This blog entry has all the details you need to know about the Ravana Cave.
